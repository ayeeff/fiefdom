The new figures highlight a growing reliance on non-traditional powerhouse states for economic growth:

  • ACT: Was the strongest performer in 2024-25 with 3.5% GSP growth, driven in part by a strong public service sector and federal election spending. Since 2000, its economy has expanded the most, by 156%.
  • Queensland (QLD): Grew by 2.2% in 2024-25 and has expanded by 125% since 2000, confirming its growing economic importance.
  • Western Australia (WA): Grew by 1.3% in 2024-25 and has expanded by 150% since 2000, though its economy shrank by 1.1% in per capita terms in 2024-25.
  • New South Wales (NSW): Was the slowest-growing economy in 2024-25 at just 0.9% and has the worst performance since 2000, expanding by only 65%. Its economy also went backward by 0.3% per capita.
  • Victoria (VIC): Grew by 1.1% in 2024-25, and expanded by 90% since 2000, with a per capita contraction of 0.8%.
  • Other Jurisdictions (TAS, SA, NT): All expanded by 1.0% in 2024-25.
