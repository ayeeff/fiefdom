The Strategic Trilemma

China didn't just mine rare earthsâ€”it engineered a full-stack supply chain monopoly while the West was distracted. From ore to magnet, China controls 90% of refining and 92% of the highest-value production stage. With 26,000 rare earth patents filed (1950-2019) versus only 9,810 in the U.S., China has established technological supremacy.

The December 2025 export rules extend this control to foreign-made magnets containing even trace Chinese material. Every Virginia-class boat needs ~4.6 tons of REEs; each Columbia-class needs over 5 tons of sintered NdFeB magnets for propulsion and silent-running systems. No Western plant can yet produce these at scale with non-Chinese feedstock.

Bottom Line: The West is trying to replicate in under 10 years what took China over 20 years, under stricter environmental rules, higher labor costs, and without the scientific-industrial base China accumulated. If magnet and heavy REE separation capacity is not operationally proven by 2028, the Columbia-class schedule slips, the SSBN gap widens, and extended deterrence commitments become mutually exclusive.
