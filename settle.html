Historical Migration Routes to Australia

Route Information: Historical Voyages to Australia

First Fleet (1787–1788)

  • Duration: 252 days (~15,000–16,000 nautical miles)
  • Fleet: 11 ships
  • Mortality: ~3%
  • Details: Carefully planned voyage with multiple resupply stops at Tenerife, Rio de Janeiro, and Cape Town. Strong leadership by Captain Arthur Phillip ensured humane treatment and adequate provisions, resulting in low mortality.

Second Fleet (1790) & Chinese Gold Miners (1850s–1860s)

Second Fleet (1790)

  • Duration: 158–160 days (~18,000–20,000 nautical miles)
  • Fleet: 6 ships (private contractors)
  • Mortality: ~26% (~160 deaths)
  • Details: Known as the “Death Fleet.” Poorly managed voyage marked by deliberate starvation, overcrowding, and brutality. Convicts were kept chained below deck with minimal rations.

Chinese Gold Miners (1850s–1860s)

  • Duration: 3–5 months (~10,500 km total)
  • Migrants: ~17,000 via Robe, South Australia
  • Mortality: 5–10%
  • Details: Sailed from Guangzhou or Hong Kong, often around the Roaring Forties to reach South Australia, then forced to trek over 500 km (“Robe Walk”) to the Victorian goldfields to avoid discriminatory taxes. Faced severe hardship and racism during the journey.
