Historical Migration Routes to Australia

Route Information: Historical Voyages to Australia

First Fleet (1787–1788)

  • Duration: 252 days (~15,000–16,000 nm)
  • Fleet: 11 ships
  • Mortality: ~3%
  • Details: Planned stops at Tenerife, Rio, and Cape Town; Phillip's leadership ensured humane care and low losses.

Second Fleet (1790)

  • Duration: 158–160 days (~18,000–20,000 nm)
  • Fleet: 6 ships (private)
  • Mortality: ~26% (~160 deaths)
  • Details: "Death Fleet": starvation, abuse, and chaining led to high fatalities.

Chinese Gold Miners (1850s–1860s)

  • Duration: 3–5 months (~10,500 km)
  • Migrants: ~17,000 via Robe
  • Mortality: 5–10%
  • Details: From Guangzhou via Roaring Forties to Robe; 500km trek to goldfields amid taxes and racism.
